Geography Notes For UPSC - Geography is a crucial subject in the IAS exam, it is covered in both the IAS Prelims and Mains papers. Geography for UPSC includes both Indian and global geography. Geography is distinct from other science subjects, but it is closely related to them. This subject collects and synthesizes data from all-natural and social sciences. Furthermore, the Geography Notes for UPSC will be useful to candidates during their last-minute exam preparations.
To understand difficult topics further, it is necessary to lay a solid foundation in Geography by studying NCERT books from Class VIII to Class XII. They assist in covering all of the fundamentals and basics on all topics in a simple and easy language.
Once you’ve mastered the fundamentals, read the following books to gain a thorough understanding of geography:
Goh Cheng Lehong’s Physical and Human Geography Certificate, D R Khullar’s India: A Comprehensive Geography, and Savindra Singh’s Physical Geography.
Be a wise candidate by attempting to connect the static portion of the Geography subject with current events. There will be a lot of news and editorials that are directly related to the subject. As a result, keeping up with current events through newspapers is unavoidable.
Maps are an essential part of Geography, and the majority of the questions are either directly or indirectly related to them. As a result, a thorough understanding of Maps (India and World) location is essential in order to score well.
Make a habit of jotting down important topics while preparing. Having self-prepared notes will save you valuable time in the days leading up to the exam. Also, make it a habit to go over everything you’ve learned on a regular basis. It will assist you in remembering information for a longer period of time.

The making of the Constitution of India was a historic and transformative process that reflected the aspirations, diversity, and democratic spirit of the Indian people. The Constituent Assembly, formed under the Cabinet Mission Plan of 1946, was entrusted with the task of framing a Constitution for an independent India. The Constitution-makers carefully studied constitutional systems from across the world and engaged in extensive debates and discussions to create a framework suited to India’s unique social, cultural, and political conditions. The process involved the participation of eminent leaders, jurists, administrators, and visionaries who sought to establish a sovereign, democratic, republic founded on justice, liberty, equality, and fraternity.
